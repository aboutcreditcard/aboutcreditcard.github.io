The Connection Between Credit Scores and Insurance Premiums

You might wonder: Why does my credit score affect my insurance rates? The answer lies in risk assessment. Insurance companies use credit-based insurance scores (CBIS) to predict the likelihood of future claims. Studies show that individuals with higher credit scores tend to file fewer claims, making them less risky to insure.

How Credit Scores Influence Different Types of Insurance

  1. Auto Insurance – Drivers with poor credit may pay up to 91% more than those with excellent credit.
  2. Homeowners Insurance – A low score could increase premiums by 20-50%.
  3. Renters Insurance – Even renters aren’t exempt; credit health impacts their rates too.

The Global Context: Why Credit Health Matters More Than Ever

Inflation and Insurance Costs

With inflation driving up the cost of claims, insurers are tightening underwriting standards. A strong credit score can be your best defense against skyrocketing premiums.

The Rise of Usage-Based Insurance (UBI)

Many insurers now offer telematics-based discounts, but these often still factor in credit scores. Credit Dyno ensures you qualify for the best possible rates, whether traditional or UBI-based.

Common Myths About Credit and Insurance

Myth 1: “Insurance Companies Don’t Care About Credit in My State”

While some states (like California and Massachusetts) restrict the use of credit in auto insurance, most still allow it for homeowners and other policies.

Myth 2: “Checking My Credit Will Hurt My Score”

Credit Dyno uses soft inquiries, which don’t affect your score, to provide insights.

Myth 3: “I Can’t Improve My Score Quickly”

With targeted strategies (e.g., paying down high-utilization cards, disputing errors), significant improvements can happen in as little as 30-60 days.
